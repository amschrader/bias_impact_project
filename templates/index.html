HOW IT WORKS

This simulation creates an eight-level company with an equal number of women and men at each employee level. Level one is entry-level and level eight represents the executive level.

The simulator completes 20 promotional cycles, which represents 2 performance reviews per year for 10 years.

A 15% attrition rate is applied to every promotional cycle by removing 15% of the lowest ranking employees from all employee levels.

Retained employees are randomly assigned performance-review scores from 1-100, or 1-100 + bias amount for the favored gender. Each new score is added to that employee’s previous performance-review scores.

Note: The simulator uses random selection for performance-review scores, so the results will have a small variance.

The simulation was inspired by "Male-Female Differences: A Computer Simulation" and “From bias to exclusion: A multilevel emergent theory of gender segregation in organizations” and illustrates the impact of bias on a simplified dataset.
